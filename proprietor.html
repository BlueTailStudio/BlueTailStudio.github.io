The Proprietor

Designation: N█████ R███  ·  Known alias: N.R.
Entity Contingent Active since 1830 Historical · Philosophical Public — updated 2016
01 · Overview

The subject designated PC · 1830 · ENT · 167, referred to within this record as The Proprietor, is an entity of unknown origin first confirmed by the Archive in 1830 following a mass casualty event at a Salon establishment in ████████, Midwest region of the United States. The subject presents as a human male of indeterminate age, operating as the proprietor and sole staff member of whatever establishment it currently inhabits.

Current evidence suggests the subject is not separate from the establishment it occupies, but constitutes a single unified entity — the building, its contents, and its front-facing human form functioning as integrated components of one organism. The subject's human form acts as its means of communication and guest management. The establishment itself serves as its body.

The subject has been observed across multiple decades and locations, each time occupying a different form of lodging establishment consistent with the dominant travel culture of the era. It is not known whether the subject selects these forms deliberately or reflects them instinctively. No theological classification applies. The subject confirmed in interview that it holds no understanding of religious frameworks when directly questioned.

Roadside motel. Exterior consistent with mid-20th century American motor lodge.

02 · Caution Notes
Read before any field approach
Primary trigger — confirmed
The subject is believed to become dangerous when its internal order — specifically its rules regarding room assignment and guest conduct — is violated. The 1830 mass casualty event was determined to have been triggered by guests disregarding assigned lodging arrangements. The subject does not warn. Response is immediate and without observable emotional activation.
Property integrity — confirmed
Members must not damage the physical structure of the establishment in any way. Testing in 1831 confirmed that damage to the building provokes an extreme response. The subject changes form when this occurs. Members who conduct property tests must do so outside the premises. Note: the building has been observed to pursue members who remove structural material. Pursuit ceases when civilian witnesses are present.
Overnight stay — avoid
The subject consistently and insistently offers accommodation to visiting members. Under no circumstances should Chronicle members accept. The subject does not show aggression when refused, but the offer will be repeated. The reason for this insistence remains under investigation.
Premises boundary
The subject has not been observed to leave the boundary of its establishment voluntarily. Pursuit of members who removed structural material in 1831 is the single recorded exception, and was abandoned when pedestrians entered the area. Interviews may be conducted safely at the threshold of the premises.
03 · Historical Record
1830
Subject first confirmed operating as proprietor of a Salon establishment in ████████, Midwest. 36 deaths recorded over a period of weeks, attributed by local law enforcement to gang violence. Archive cross-referenced Sheriff's report with anomalous behavioral patterns and opened this case. The subject was the only individual present across all incident periods who was not listed among the dead or missing.
1831
Archive dispatched a mixed Historical and Philosophical team to the Salon following conclusion of the 1830 incident. Members concealed identities and institutional affiliation. Interview conducted — see transcript below. Post-interview structural testing conducted outside premises. Subject pursued member carrying removed wall material for approximately 40 meters before abandoning pursuit upon encountering pedestrian traffic. Subject showed no further aggression.
1831 — approx.
Following the 1831 contact, the Archive maintained periodic observation of the Salon. No further mass casualty events were recorded in the period. Guest population remained low. Subject behavior consistent with earlier observation.
Late 1800s
The Salon ceased operation. Archive records the subject's status as Missing for this period. Cross-reference with PC · ████ · ENT · ███ for possible sightings during this interval — connection unconfirmed.
1925
A roadside motel opened near ████████, California. Reports of time displacement among guests reached the Archive via a law enforcement correspondent. Status updated from Missing to Active. A team was not dispatched at this stage due to resource constraints and uncertainty of identification.
1942
A team of volunteers sourced from an external organization operating under similar parameters was trained by Archive Historical staff and dispatched to the motel for a structured three-day stay. The team did not return. Their records were recovered and are included below. One member continued returning to the establishment voluntarily until death by dehydration and starvation. The subject attempted to prevent Archive staff from recovering the body, stating the individual was "still a guest." No aggression was displayed during the retrieval.
1955
Nine deaths recorded at the motel. Local law enforcement investigation inconclusive. Archive conducted second interview with the subject. See transcript below. An unidentified civilian, origin year unknown, was present during the incident. This individual was not confirmed among the deceased and their current whereabouts and time of origin remain unknown. Interview with this individual has not been possible.
2001
See Civilian Incident section below.
2016 — present
This record was updated to public access status in 2016 following the subject's reappearance in a new location and the increased risk to uninformed civilians. An Archive research team has been stationed in ████████, California for passive observation. No direct contact has been initiated as of this record. A new pattern of guest experience has been observed and is currently under active investigation.

INCIDENT TYPE: Mass casualty / suspected gang violence
LOCATION: ████████ Salon, ████████ St., ████████
TOTAL DECEASED: 36
PERIOD: Approx. ██ weeks

Deaths occurred across multiple incidents over the recorded period. Cause of death varied. No single consistent method of killing was identified, which led initial investigators to suspect multiple perpetrators operating within or associated with regional gang activity.

The proprietor of the Salon, a ████████ male, was interviewed on ██/██/1830. He was cooperative and showed no distress. He stated that all individuals had been guests of the establishment and that he had assigned each to their rooms as was customary. He expressed no reaction when informed of the death toll.

No arrests were made. Case attributed to gang activity and closed on ██/██/1830.

05 · Interview Transcript — 1831
Post-incident · Salon premises · Identities concealed

Interview conducted at the threshold of the Salon premises approximately three months after the 1830 incident. Team members concealed their faces and did not disclose organizational affiliation. The subject was cooperative throughout and showed no awareness of or concern for the identities of those present. The primary concern expressed by the subject during the interview was whether the visiting members would be requiring rooms.

DATE: ██/██/1831  ·  LOCATION: ████████ Salon, threshold  ·  TEAM: Historical (lead) · Philosophical  ·  SUBJECT: The Proprietor
Staff #0014
Good afternoon. We are travelers passing through. We had some questions about the establishment, if you would permit.
Subject
Of course. Will you be needing rooms?
Staff #0014
Not at this time, thank you. We understand there was some trouble here recently. A number of deaths.
Subject
There was. Are you sure you would not like a room? I have several available.
Staff #0016
We appreciate the offer. Perhaps we can speak first. Can you tell us what happened?
Subject
Guests were placed in their rooms. Some of them did not stay in their rooms. A couple — they had their own room, each of them — but one stayed only in the other's room. That room was not their room.
Staff #0016
And the other deaths?
Subject
Some guests saw. Some guests said things. The rooms were not being used correctly.
[No further elaboration offered. Subject did not appear to consider this insufficient as an explanation.]
Staff #0014
Do you feel anything about what happened?
Subject
I feel that the rooms are now empty.
Staff #0016
We have heard that some believe you to be something other than human. A demon, perhaps, or something of a religious nature.
Subject
I don't know what those are.
Staff #0016
Are you human?
Subject
I am the proprietor.
[Pause. Subject looked toward the interior of the Salon before continuing.]
Subject
Are you sure you would not like rooms? I also have girls available, if that is of interest. They attend to guests in the evenings.
[Referring to the Salon's service women — women employed in entertainment and companionship roles common to frontier Salon establishments of the period. Offer made without change in tone or apparent meaning.]
Staff #0014
No, thank you. One last question. Did you grow up here? Do you have family?
Subject
I had a mother. A father. We each had our own spaces. That is how it should be.
[Subject did not elaborate further and redirected the conversation once more to the availability of rooms. Interview concluded.]

Following the interview, Staff #0016 conducted a structural test outside the premises, removing a small quantity of wood from an exterior wall panel. The subject immediately changed form — a full description is held in the restricted addendum to this file — and pursued the staff member for approximately 40 meters. Pursuit ceased when three pedestrians entered the street. The staff member returned to the Archive without injury.

06 · Operational Record — 1942
External volunteer deployment · Motel premises

Due to the assessed risk to Archive staff, a team of volunteers sourced from an external organization operating under similar parameters was engaged for this operation. Volunteers were briefed and trained in basic recording protocol by Archive Historical staff before deployment. They were not informed of the full contents of this case file.

The team checked into the motel on ██/██/1942 for a planned three-night stay. Their final log entries are reproduced below. The team did not return. Archive Historical staff recovered the written records from the premises six days later. One team member — Volunteer ████ — had continued returning to the motel voluntarily after the planned departure date. He was found deceased inside the premises on day six, cause of death determined as dehydration and starvation. The subject indicated the individual was still a registered guest and requested the body not be removed. No aggression was displayed. The body was retrieved.

TEAM: 4 volunteers (external organization) · TRAINED BY: Staff #0029 (Historical) · PLANNED STAY: 3 nights · OUTCOME: Team lost / one body recovered
Day 1 · Evening · Volunteer ████ (lead recorder)
Checked in without incident. Proprietor assigned rooms — one per person, no deviation permitted. He asked twice whether we would like additional services. We declined. Rooms are clean. Older than expected for a motel of this age. The proprietor watches the hallway from the front desk. Can see him from the gap under the door.
Day 2 · Morning · Volunteer ████
Something happened last night. I cannot explain it clearly. I was in my room and then I was not. There were people I did not recognize — dressed differently. The building was the same building but it smelled different. Like wood smoke and animals. A woman was laughing somewhere. Then I was back in my room. I am writing this down because that is what I was told to do. The others say the same thing happened to them. We are discussing whether to leave.
Day 2 · Afternoon · Volunteer ████
We did not leave. ████ said he wanted one more night. He said there was a woman in the other place who he needed to speak to again. The proprietor gave us dinner. He seemed pleased we were staying.
Day 3 · Morning · Volunteer ████ (lead recorder — others not present)
I cannot find the others. The proprietor says they went back to their rooms. I looked. They are not in their rooms. There was blood in the hallway that was not from this time. I think it was from the other place. I am leaving now. I am leaving this log at the front desk. The proprietor asked me why I was leaving early. I told him I had to go. He said my room was still available. He said it in the same voice as when I arrived.
Additional note — recovered separately · Handwriting identified as Volunteer ████
I know how it ends for her. If I go back I can stop it. I know I can stop it this time. The room is still there. He said the room is still there.
07 · Interview Transcript — 1955
Post-incident · Motel premises · Historical · Philosophical team

Interview conducted following the 1955 incident. Theological representation was not included in this team, as the theological classification had been ruled out following the 1831 interview. The subject's manner was notably more adapted to human conversational convention than in 1831, though the Archive cannot confirm whether this reflects change in the subject or improved mimicry. The subject's name — N.R. — was self-assigned. Full name is redacted in this public record to prevent cognitive re-attachment in former guests.

DATE: ██/██/1955  ·  LOCATION: Motel threshold, ████████, California  ·  TEAM: Historical (lead) · Philosophical  ·  NO THEOLOGICAL MEMBER (ruled out, 1831)
Staff #0038
Can you tell us your name?
Subject
N.R. Will you be needing rooms?
Staff #0038
Not tonight. We wanted to ask about what happened here. Nine people died.
Subject
They were not in their rooms.
Staff #0041
You mean they were in the wrong rooms?
Subject
A room is a room. Everyone has their own. That is the arrangement.
Staff #0041
Do you understand that what you did is considered a crime? That killing people is considered wrong?
Subject
I understand that people say so.
[No further response. Subject appeared to consider the question settled.]
Staff #0038
There was a guest who cannot be accounted for. Someone who was in the register but not among the dead. Do you know where that person is?
Subject
He checked in. He was in his room.
Staff #0038
Was. Is he still here?
Subject
He left. Guests leave.
[Subject paused briefly before adding:]
Subject
Most of them.
Staff #0041
We spoke with someone like you once. A long time ago. At a Salon, not a motel. Was that you?
Subject
I have always been here.
Staff #0041
That person mentioned their family. A mother. A father. Each in their own space.
Subject
Yes.
[No elaboration. Subject showed no recognition of the implication that this confirmed continuity of identity across 124 years.]
Staff #0038
Are you human?
Subject
I am the proprietor.
[Identical response to 1831 interview. No change in phrasing or affect. Interview concluded. Subject reminded team once more that rooms were available.]
08 · Civilian Incident — 2001
Unidentified civilian · Origin year unknown

In 2001, an unidentified civilian with no known connection to the Archive or any affiliated organization was recorded as a guest at the motel. The individual's name provided at check-in could not be verified. Their origin year is unknown. Cross-referencing of their physical description, speech patterns, and personal effects observed during the stay suggests possible temporal displacement, though this has not been confirmed.

The individual was not among any recorded casualties. Their current whereabouts are unknown. No interview has been possible. The subject (N.R.) has made no comment on this individual beyond confirming they checked in and checked out.

09 · Staff Commentary
Appended in order · Not edited · Not removed
Staff #0041 · 2001
"The civilian incident changes things. This is the first time a non-Archive, non-volunteer individual has been confirmed inside the premises and survived — as far as we know. We don't know where they came from or where they went. We don't know if they're still alive. We don't know what year they think it is. Adding cross-reference flag for temporal displacement cases."
Staff #0029 · 2003
"I trained the 1942 team. I have thought about Volunteer ████ for sixty years. His note was in his own handwriting. He was not confused or coerced. He went back because he wanted to. The room was still there. It is always still there. I do not think we should be sending anyone else in."
Staff #0055 · 2009
"Cross-referencing 1831 structural test results with current architectural surveys of the motel. The building's footprint changes. Not drastically. But the east wall is not where it was in the 1955 police photographs. It has moved approximately 1.2 meters. This is not settling or subsidence. Flagging for physical investigation."
Staff #0038 · 2016
"Recommending this record be made public. The motel is active again and in a new location. Civilian exposure risk is too high to keep this internal only. The public record should not include the subject's full name — re-attachment risk for anyone who has stayed there is real. Redaction of N.R.'s full name approved by Archive senior staff."
Staff #0062 · 2016
"Team stationed as of this month. Passive observation only per caution notes. The motel is operating normally from the outside. Guests check in. Some check out. We are counting the ones who don't. The proprietor waves at us from the window sometimes. Same face. Same wave."
Staff #0062 · 2019
"The loop has changed. The 1942 team described the same six-day experience — same people, same sequence. Guests coming out now are describing something different. The people inside are not the same. The sequence is different. Either the 1955 event changed something, or the civilian did. We don't know yet. Research ongoing."
Staff #0041 · 2023
"I have been on this case since 1955. I am retiring this year. I want it on record that in seventy years, the proprietor has not changed. Not one thing. Same answer to the same question. Same offer of a room. I do not believe he remembers us between visits. I do not believe he is capable of it. I also do not believe that makes him less dangerous."
