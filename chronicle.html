παράδοξα χρονικόν  ·  Things Beyond Expectation, Recorded

We do not pursue. We do not contain.
We bear witness, and we write it down.

The Paradoxa Chronicle is a record-keeping body of historians, philosophers, and theologians united by a single conviction: that the world contains events which no single tradition; scientific, religious, or otherwise has been sufficient to explain. Our purpose is not to explain them. Our purpose is to ensure they are never forgotten, and never falsified.

Our records stretch across centuries and languages, and we draw no conclusion between them. The Chronicle exists precisely because conclusions have, historically, been the first thing to disappear.

First tradition
Faith & scripture
Second tradition
Philosophy & reason
Third tradition
History & testimony

We are not affiliated with any government, institution, or religion — though members of each have contributed to our records. We do not act, we do not intervene. When something that should not exist leaves evidence that it does, we collect that evidence, cross-reference it against everything that came before, and add it to the Archive.

"The oldest records in the Archive are written in Hebrew. When the philosophers arrived, they translated what they could into Greek. There are entries that could not be translated. Those remain as they were."
— Archivist's preface, undated

How We Record

Every case entered into the Archive follows a fixed structure.
01  ·  Case identification
Format
PC · 399BC · ENT · 001
PCChronicle prefix
399BCYear recorded
ENTClassification
001Case Number
Older entries carry handwritten registry numbers retroactively assigned to this format. Where the original date is unknown, the year of first cross-reference is used.
Staff number
Each member is assigned a permanent staff number upon induction. All entries, updates, and commentary are attributed to a number, not a name.
Names are held separately in the membership record and are not published in case files.
Date recorded
The date the entry was first committed to the Archive. Separate from the date of the event itself, which is logged in the operational record.
02  ·  Case status
Active
Ongoing. The subject is present, occurring, or continues to be encountered.
Dormant
Not currently active, but not gone. Previous activity on record.
Closed
Interaction concluded. Outcome fully documented.
Unresolved
Interaction occurred. No conclusions could be drawn.
Missing
The subject can no longer be located. Last known record noted in file.
03  ·  Classification
What kind of thing is this
Entity
Something that exists, acts, and can be engaged with directly. May be human in appearance or origin.
Phenomenon
An event, condition, or occurrence. Includes diseases, spatial anomalies, and temporal disturbances.
Residual
Something left behind. An effect without a clearly present cause.
Theological
Maps to religious record across two or more independent traditions. Assigned alongside another class, not instead of it.
04  ·  Danger rate
Member safety priority
Passive
No recorded aggression toward Chronicle members. Standard protocols apply.
Contingent
Dangerous only under specific, documented conditions. Caution notes are mandatory for this rating and above.
Active
Will engage with members present. Mixed team deployment required. Exit strategy must be established before approach.
Absolute
Contact has not produced survivors. Entry compiled from recovered materials and third-party accounts only.
05  ·  Assigned team
Who engages and why
Religious
Deployed when the subject maps to scripture, ritual, or theological record. Includes cross-traditional comparison where applicable.
Historical
Deployed when the subject has a documented record across time. Responsible for cross-referencing prior entries and establishing timeline.
Philosophical
Deployed when the subject resists classification by existing frameworks. Responsible for first-contact questioning and behavioral documentation.
Mixed
Two or more departments assigned. The rationale for the combination is recorded in the team field. Common for Entity and Theological class cases.
06  ·  Caution notes
Purpose
Everything a member needs to know before engagement. Behavioral patterns, environmental conditions, known triggers, and any factors that have previously affected member safety. Mandatory for Contingent rating and above.
Caution notes are updated after every field interaction. Outdated notes are archived, not deleted.
07  ·  Operational record
Purpose
A firsthand account of what the assigned team did, what they encountered, and what the outcome was. Written by the recording staff member upon return. Includes who was present, what was observed, and the condition of the team at conclusion.
Where members did not return, the operational record is left incomplete and marked accordingly. It is never fabricated.
08  ·  Staff commentary
Any member with Archive access may leave commentary on an open case. Commentary is timestamped and attributed to a staff number. It is not edited or removed — only appended to.
Staff #0041  ·  example entry
"Cross-reference PC-1203-UNK-007 — behavioral pattern is consistent. I believe these are the same subject."
Staff #0009  ·  later entry
"Confirmed."
Staff #0071  ·  later entry
"Status updated to Missing as of this date. We need to find it."
09  ·  Cross-references
Case IDs linked by the recording staff or added through commentary. No explanation is required in the cross-reference field itself — context is held in the respective files. A case may accumulate cross-references across centuries.
